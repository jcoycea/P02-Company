About Company

We are at the forefront of the slow fashion revolution. Analogous to the recently shifted food paradigm, we insist on the most premium and transparent methods of garment creation. We employ a hybrid of artisan-care, environment-sensitivity and local machine-power. By incorporating eco-friendly textiles, natural dyeing and American manufacturing, each Olderbrother garment is subtly unique. At the end of the garments’ lifetime, it can be buried in your backyard.

Founders

Bobby Bonaparte & Max Kingery

Bonaparte and Kingery met each other in 2013. And then, they came together as business partners just as serendipitously. They are both Bonaparte and Kingery had grown up in Portland, Ore.

They quietly construct their whimsical unisex clothes from sustainably harvested fabrics like organic Japanese cotton and use vivid dyes made from almost exclusively edible pigments. In conceiving each season’s limited-run colorways, they draw inspiration from ingredients that are animating the wellness world at that particular moment—fiery-orange turmeric, golden saffron, the muted earth tones of healing mushrooms—and spend months of R&D figuring out how to best translate those hues to evocative, relatable garments that integrate seamlessly into people’s lives.
